### Topic Recap: Relational Algebra Basics (Select, Project, Rename)

**Relational Algebra Operations**:
- **Select (sigma)**: `sigma_condition(R)` — selects rows satisfying condition.
- **Project (pi)**: `pi_attributes(R)` — selects specific columns.
- **Rename (rho)**: `rho_new_name(R)` — renames relation or attributes.

**Notation**:
- `sigma_condition(R)` — condition can use =, <, >, <=, >=, <>, AND, OR, NOT
- `pi_A1, A2, ..., An(R)` — list of attributes to keep
- `rho_S(R)` — rename relation R to S

### Problem 1: Select Operation

Given relation `Student(RollNo, Name, Age, Dept, GPA)`, write relational algebra for:

(a) Students in CS department
(b) Students with GPA >= 8.0
(c) CS students with GPA >= 8.0
(d) Students not in CS or EE

**Solution**:

(a) `sigma_Dept='CS'(Student)`
(b) `sigma_GPA >= 8.0(Student)`
(c) `sigma_Dept='CS' AND GPA >= 8.0(Student)`
(d) `sigma_NOT(Dept='CS' OR Dept='EE')(Student)` or `sigma_Dept <> 'CS' AND Dept <> 'EE'(Student)`

### Problem 2: Project Operation

Given `Student(RollNo, Name, Age, Dept, GPA, City)`, write RA for:

(a) List all department names
(b) List student names and GPAs
(c) List unique departments

**Solution**:

(a) `pi_Dept(Student)` — may have duplicates. For unique: need to mention that RA eliminates duplicates by default.
(b) `pi_Name, GPA(Student)`
(c) `pi_Dept(Student)` — duplicates eliminated automatically in RA

### Problem 3: Combining Select and Project

For `Student(RollNo, Name, Age, Dept, GPA, City)`:

(a) Find names and GPAs of CS students
(b) Find roll numbers of students from Delhi
(c) Find names of students with GPA > 9.0

**Solution**:

(a) `pi_Name, GPA(sigma_Dept='CS'(Student))`
(b) `pi_RollNo(sigma_City='Delhi'(Student))`
(c) `pi_Name(sigma_GPA > 9.0(Student))`

### Problem 4: Rename Operation

Given `Employee(EID, Name, Salary, DeptID)`:

(a) Rename Employee to Emp
(b) Rename EID to EmployeeID and Name to EmpName
(c) Find pairs of employees who work in the same department (use rename for self-join)

**Solution**:

(a) `rho_Emp(Employee)`
(b) `rho_(EmployeeID, EmpName, Salary, DeptID)(Employee)` — renames all attributes
(c) Self-join using rename:
```
rho_E1(Employee)
rho_E2(Employee)
pi_E1.Name, E2.Name(sigma_E1.DeptID = E2.DeptID AND E1.EID < E2.EID(E1 x E2))
```

### Problem 5: Complex Conditions

For `Employee(EID, Name, Salary, DeptID, HireYear)`:

Find names of employees hired after 2020 with salary > 50000, in department D1 or D2.

**Solution**:

`pi_Name(sigma_HireYear > 2020 AND Salary > 50000 AND (DeptID='D1' OR DeptID='D2')(Employee))`

Alternatively:

`pi_Name(sigma_HireYear > 2020 AND Salary > 50000 AND DeptID IN ('D1', 'D2')(Employee))`
