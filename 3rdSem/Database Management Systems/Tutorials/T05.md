## Notes

### Topic Recap: Relational Algebra Advanced Operations, Relational Calculus

**Additional RA Operations**:
- **Union (U)**: R U S — tuples in R or S (compatible schemas).
- **Set Difference (-)**: R - S — tuples in R but not in S.
- **Intersection (n)**: R n S — tuples in both (derived: R - (R - S)).
- **Cartesian Product (x)**: R x S — all pairs of tuples.
- **Join conditions**: theta-join, equi-join, natural join, outer join.
- **Division (/)**: R / S — tuples in R matching all tuples in S.

**Relational Calculus**: Declarative (what, not how).
- **Tuple Relational Calculus (TRC)**: `{t | condition(t)}`
- **Domain Relational Calculus (DRC)**: `{<a1, a2, ...> | condition}`

### Problem 1: Union, Difference, Intersection

Given `Student2025(RollNo, Name, Dept)` and `Student2026(RollNo, Name, Dept)`:

(a) Students enrolled in either 2025 or 2026
(b) Students enrolled in both years
(c) Students enrolled in 2025 but not 2026

**Solution**:

(a) `Student2025 U Student2026`
(b) `Student2025 n Student2026` (or `Student2025 - (Student2025 - Student2026)`)
(c) `Student2025 - Student2026`

### Problem 2: Cartesian Product and Join

Given `Student(RollNo, Name, Dept)` and `Enrollment(RollNo, CourseCode, Grade)`:

(a) Find all possible student-course pairs (Cartesian product)
(b) Find students with their enrollments (equi-join on RollNo)
(c) Find students who have enrolled in at least one course

**Solution**:

(a) `Student x Enrollment` — warning: many meaningless pairs
(b) `Student Join_RollNo Enrollment` — natural join on common attribute RollNo

Or theta-join: `Student Join_Student.RollNo = Enrollment.RollNo Enrollment`

(c) The join itself produces only matching pairs. The result includes only students with enrollments.

### Problem 3: Division Operation

Given `EnrolledStudent(RollNo, CourseCode)` and `CSCourses(CourseCode)`:

Find students enrolled in ALL CS courses.

**Solution**:

`EnrolledStudent / CSCourses`

This returns RollNo values that have an enrollment record for every CourseCode in CSCourses.

**Manual derivation**:
- Step 1: Find all student-course pairs: `pi_RollNo, CourseCode(EnrolledStudent) x CSCourses`
- Step 2: Subtract actual enrollments: `(Step 1) - EnrolledStudent`
- Step 3: Students missing some CS course: `pi_RollNo(Step 2)`
- Step 4: Students enrolled in all: `pi_RollNo(EnrolledStudent) - Step 3`

### Problem 4: Outer Joins

Given `Student(RollNo, Name, Dept)` and `Enrollment(RollNo, CourseCode)`:

(a) Left outer join: All students, with their enrollments (if any)
(b) Right outer join: All enrollments, with student names (if any)
(c) Full outer join: All students and all enrollments matched where possible

**Solution**:

(a) `Student LeftOuterJoin Enrollment` — includes students with no enrollments (CourseCode = NULL)
(b) `Student RightOuterJoin Enrollment` — includes enrollments with no matching student (Name = NULL)
(c) `Student FullOuterJoin Enrollment` — both sides preserved

### Problem 5: Tuple Relational Calculus

Express in TRC: "Find names of students who have GPA > 8.0"

**Solution**:

`{t.Name | Student(t) AND t.GPA > 8.0}`

"Find names of students enrolled in course CS301":

`{t.Name | Student(t) AND EXISTS e (Enrollment(e) AND e.RollNo = t.RollNo AND e.CourseCode = 'CS301')}`

"Find names of students enrolled in ALL CS courses":

`{t.Name | Student(t) AND FORALL c (CSCourse(c) => EXISTS e (Enrollment(e) AND e.RollNo = t.RollNo AND e.CourseCode = c.CourseCode))}`
